Engineering Artifact - Water Monitoring System

For the engineering team artifact we where placed into teams, I was tasked with being team comunications lead. The task we were given was to creating a water blockage detection system. This project was a response to a professional brief from Network Rail to develop a robust, retrofit monitoring system for culverts. The client required a reliable and cost-effective method to track water levels and detect debris in hard-to-reach drainage areas to prevent critical infrastructure failure. The task demanded a resilient, low-maintenance solution capable of operating in harsh, confined environments to provide real-time data without the need for constant manual intervention.

The finished prototype water monitoring system

The system was designed to be mounted to the vast majority of culvert with fully adjustable brackets. The idea we came up with as a team was to have 2 ultrasonic sensors - one before and one after, as well as a microcontroller to compare the difference in the water levels. It would then calculate if there was a large difference in level. If there was, a picture would be taken through a camera (with an LED for light). This would then send a message through an email or a website to warn Network Rail of a blocked culvert. The system would be enclosed in a sealed platic pipe to allow for full waterproofing and protection. I would have a vent plug to allow for even pressure and the opening would be sealed for full waterproofing. The system would be powered with a trackside power supply cable through a transformer.

Creating this water monitoring system involved working with the Raspberry Pi Pico microcontroller and sensor. The Pi was going to be a Pico 2 W but this was unavailable so the website idea was not used. Instead, we used a Pico and connected the 2 ultrasonic sensors. The camera unfortunatley was unable to be used but placed in the theory of operation. The microcontroller was then coded and tested to evaluate its effectiveness.
